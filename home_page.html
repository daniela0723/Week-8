About

Lazaro Cardenas is a small port city on the Pacific Coast, in the southwest part of the state of Michoacan, Mexico. It has a population size < 100,000 (80, 700 in 2014). Founded in 1931, the city was named after former Mexican president Lazaro Cardenas Del Rio who served from 1934 - 1940, and is best known for agrarian reform and nationalizing the oil industry.

The municipality of Lazaro Cardenas is made up of the city of Lazaro Cardenas and neighboring communities of Las Guacamayas, La Orilla, and La Mira. It's current mayor is Itze Camacho Zapiain.

The Port of Lazaro Cardenas is a deep-water seaport that handles container, dry-bulk. The port is 18 meters deep (59 feet), the deepest in all of Mexico, and is able to receive some of the biggest container ships in the world. Port of Lazaro Cardenas is one of the largest sea ports in all of Mexico and a serves as the country's gateway to important markets in Asia.

Climate

The climate in in Lazaro Cardenas is hot and very humid. The rain season lasts 4 months from June to October, overlapping with the hurricane season which runs from May through November. Tropical storms and the occasional hurricane impact the city every year.

The best time of the year to visit the city is late December to late May, when average temperatures range from 62° to 81° Farenheit.

How To Get There

The city has a small airport with limited flights to Mexico City. The nearest international airport is localed in Ixtapa/Zihuatanejo, Guerreo, to the south. International travelers can also access the General Francisco Mujica International Airport, just outside of the capital city of Morelia, Michoacan. Additional bus travel is required to go to Lazaro Cardenas from Morelia and Ixtapa/Zihuatanejo.
