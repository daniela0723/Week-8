Local Attractions

Malecon de la Cultura y las Artes

A 0.43 mile-long pier built along the River Balsas open 6 days a week from 6:30 to 10:00 pm. There you will find a bike trail, exercise equipment, a playground, pic nic tables, soda fountains, an outdoor theater, and a restaurant. The Malecon is busiest over the weekend and has become the go-to recreational spot for local families.

Albatross Bridge

The bridge was inaugurated in 2010. The entire bridge is 15.5 miles long. The bridge allows the passage of ships up to 79 feet wide. The bridge can be seen from Malecon de La Cultura y Las Artes.

Beaches

Just minutes outside of Lazaro Cardenas one can find several beaches: Playa Erendira, Playa Jardin, and Playa Azul.

Playa Azul

Playa Azul is a town that caters to tourists who flock to its beach every year for Spring Break and over the winter holidays. The town is best known for its cultural and culinary events. Every year, the beach town hosts an exposition for sea turtles called Expo Tortuga in which participants can help release baby sea turtles into the sea. This even is meant to raise awareness about this endangered species.

Barra de Pichi

En route to Playa Azul there is a crocodile-infested estuary called Barra de Pichi. Tourists have been know to swim these placid, shallow waters despite warnings from local authorities. Most people remain on the shore and only get close enough to the water to take a photo of the crocodiles.

Expo Feria

The annual fair takes place from March to April. Schools and other associations participate in a large parade that takes place in the fair's innaugural parade. The main attraction at the fair are the entertainers (musical groups, singers, and comedians). Rides, carnival games, shops, and food stands are set up throughout the fair grounds.

Shopping Centers

Plaza Las Americas

Cool down at Plaza Las Americas, a shopping complex made up of stores and fast-food restaurants. The main building is a one-story shopping mall which also has a movie theater.

Tianguis Sobreruedas

A tianguis is a colorful and bustling outdoor market where one can shop for fresh food, dry goods, home goods, toys, shoes, and clothing. The local tianguis will set up on Fridays and Saturdays in different locations around town. Here one can shop for groceries and enjoy a meal with the family.
